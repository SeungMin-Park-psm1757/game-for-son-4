# Soft Hand-Painted Care Art

Create original 2D game art that feels soft, warm, nature-rich, and animation-like without copying any named studio or franchise look directly.

## Core Direction

- Prefer hand-painted storybook energy over retro pixel structure.
- Keep silhouettes simple enough to read on a phone at a glance.
- Use rounded forms, gentle line weight, soft cel shading, and watercolor-like gradients.
- Let the pet face carry the emotion first, not the prop count.
- Keep the world calm: one main light source, one mood accent, limited background clutter.

## Visual Pillars

1. Make the pet feel kind, curious, and alive.
2. Make rooms and stage props feel cozy without stealing focus from care actions.
3. Make every icon readable in dense mobile UI.

## Palette Rules

- Base the world on airy sky blue, leaf mint, warm straw, cream, clay pink, and muted indigo.
- Use saturation sparingly.
- Reserve the brightest accents for rewards, affection, healing, and magical highlights.
- Keep shadows colored, not gray. Favor blue-gray, moss, and plum shadows.

## Shape Rules

- Build with large soft shapes first, then add a few charm details.
- Avoid tiny texture noise.
- Prefer asymmetry that feels natural, not messy.
- Make props slightly oversized and rounded so they stay legible in small cards.

## Rendering Rules

- For SVG icons, draw at 512x512 or larger, then display smaller in UI.
- Use 3 value bands: base, warm highlight, soft shadow.
- Add one small specular highlight or rim light when it helps readability.
- Avoid pixel grids, crisp-edge rendering, or blocky square shading.
- Use subtle drop shadows only when they separate the asset from light backgrounds.

## Pet Rules

- Keep the eyes and mouth readable before adding body detail.
- Use neck, cheek, tail, and posture to show emotion.
- Make sleep scenes quieter and softer than daytime scenes.
- Keep growth-stage changes clear through silhouette and proportion, not busy decoration.

## Background Rules

- Paint in three layers: sky, middle mood layer, ground/foreground.
- Use a few anchor props instead of many small props.
- Let sleep backgrounds feel safe, hushed, and gently luminous.
- For mobile, leave visual breathing room around the pet and speech bubble.

## Workflow

1. Read the screen context first: dock icon, menu card icon, stage prop, or full backdrop.
2. Define one silhouette sentence for the asset before drawing it.
3. Pick one main material family: leaf, wood, fabric, ceramic, moonlight, or glass.
4. Add soft highlight and colored shadow.
5. Check the result at mobile scale.
6. Remove any detail that disappears or turns muddy at small size.

## Success Criteria

- The art feels like a polished 2D care game, not a placeholder prototype.
- The pet remains the focus.
- Icons stay readable on a phone.
- Sleep scenes, UI props, and menu art all feel like the same world.
